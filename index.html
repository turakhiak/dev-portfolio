Research & Inquiry

Engaging with complex chemical problems through rigorous methodology.

Investigation into Polymer Degradation Rates

Research Question

How does pH variation affect the degradation rate of biodegradable polylactic acid (PLA) films?

Methodology

Synthesized PLA films and subjected them to controlled hydrolysis in buffer solutions ranging from pH 4 to 10 over a 4-week period. Measured mass loss and tensile strength changes.

Key Results

Demonstrated that alkaline environments significantly accelerate hydrolysis, suggesting optimal composting conditions for PLA waste management.

Synthesis of Metal-Organic Frameworks (MOFs)

Research Question

Can solvothermal synthesis be optimized to increase the surface area of Copper-based MOFs for carbon capture?

Methodology

Varied temperature and solvent ratios during synthesis. Characterized resulting crystals using X-ray diffraction (XRD) and BET surface area analysis.

Key Results

Identified a specific temperature range that maximized porosity, achieving a 15% increase in CO2 adsorption capacity compared to standard synthesis methods.

Electrochemical Efficiency in Water Splitting

Research Question

What is the effect of doping nickel electrodes with iron on the overpotential of the oxygen evolution reaction (OER)?

Methodology

Fabricated Ni-Fe alloy electrodes via electrodeposition. Conducted linear sweep voltammetry to measure current density and overpotential.

Key Results

Iron doping reduced the overpotential by 200mV at 10 mA/cm¬≤, indicating significantly improved catalytic efficiency for hydrogen production.
